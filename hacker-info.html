    Enterprise Process Center (Business Process Management Software)

    Interfacing's flagship BPM software, the Enterprise Process Center, helps companies gain a 360 degree blueprint of the organization in support of complex improvement, compliance and transformation initiatives. The EPC goes beyond process mapping & analysis, it covers the full spectrum of lifecycle governance and collaboration, enterprise architecture (EA), strategy-to-execution and workflow automation needs. The EPC offers a single repository to manage all complex business entity relationships and interdependencies, including: processes, procedures, roles, org units, resources, risks, controls, policies, rules, documents, strategic objectives, key performance indicators, systems, meta data & services. The EPC facilitates the alignment and visualization of strategic and operational performance and is often leveraged for quality, improvement, risk & audit management initiatives such as ISO, Lean, Basel III, SOX, and ITIL, amongst others.

Getting to McHacks

  • Accessibility Notice

    There is a lot of construction on Rue McTavish. If you choose to enter the SSMU building through Rue McTavish, you will have to go through metal stairs and scaffolding. As such, we strongly reccommend taking the back entrance to the SSMU building (see map below) via Rue Peel. If you have any accessibility concerns, please contact us.

  • Notes for Drivers

    Montreal has some interesting bylaws. Most important for drivers: turning right on a red light is illegal on the Island of Montreal, so don't do that.

    Parking is available for the entire weekend at McIntyre Parking garage (see map below) for $30. Please note that in/out privileges will cost an extra $10. If you want to explore alternate parking options, check out McGill's Visitor Parking Services site.

  • Travel Reimbursements

    If you filled out our reimbursement form by January 20th, you are eligible for partial travel reimbursement. Please not that we will not be providing reimbursements until after the event, and that your reimbursement is contingent on your submission of a hack.
